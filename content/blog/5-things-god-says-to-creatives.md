## 1. You weren’t just made by God. You were made like Him.

Genesis 1:27 says God created mankind in His image.

And the first thing Scripture ever tells us about God is that He created.<br>
So if you’re made in His image, creating is in you.<br>
Imagining. Designing. Building. Bringing things to life.<br>
Creativity isn’t a personality type. It’s spiritual DNA.

## 2. God filled creatives with His Spirit on purpose

Exodus 31:3–5 talks about Bezalel.<br>
A designer. A builder. A craftsman.<br>
God says He filled him with His Spirit, with wisdom, ability, and skill.

Before kings. Before prophets.<br>
God chose a creative.

Why?<br>
Because the place where God’s presence would dwell wasn’t going to be thrown together.<br>
It needed beauty, order, and excellence.<br>
God trusted creatives with His presence.

## 3. Your gift makes room for you

Proverbs 18:16 says a man’s gift makes room for him and brings him before great men.<br>
That thing you feel called to do isn’t random. It’s not extra.<br>
It’s a tool God uses to position you for provision, influence, and impact.

Your creativity is part of your assignment.

## 4. You are God’s workmanship

Ephesians 2:10 says we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works.<br>
The word workmanship comes from the word poem.

You are a masterpiece made to make things that matter.<br>
God made you on purpose, for a purpose, and that includes the things you feel called to build.

## 5. Your creativity is meant to glorify God

1 Peter 4:10–11 says we should use whatever gift we’ve received to serve others,<br>
so that in all things God may be praised through Jesus Christ.

Whether you’re filming videos, writing, designing, painting, or building something no one sees yet,<br>
when you do it with excellence and obedience,<br>
it becomes worship.

Your creativity was never meant to glorify you.<br>
It was always meant to point back to Him.
